Japanese Food

Globally known and loved for their sushi, ramen and gyozas, Japan's diverse cuisine is definitely worth further exploring! Take this chance to get a basic overview of what makes up Japanese cuisine from unknown dishes to the staple ingredients!

Japanese Food Kitchen Staples and Cooking Techniques My Favorites

Popular Japanese Dishes

Sushi

A combination of rice seasoned with rice vinegar and various ingredients, ranging from raw fish and shrimp to eggs and vegetables. As opposed to the rest of the world, Japanese people generally don't eat sushi with soy sauce!

Okonomiyaki

A savory Japanese pancake made from a batter of egg, flour and cabbage, with the addition of various ingredients according to regional tastes.

Yakitori

These delicious grilled chicken skewers are a must! They work well as a small snack or a light meal with beers, all while being quite affordable.

Soba

An old but classic dish! Buckwheat noodles that are refreshing, healthy and hold far more nutritional benefits than other noodles!

Sukiyaki

A Japanese hot pot dish, with a light and sweet flavour. Previously eaten mostly for celebrations, it is now also enjoyed as a typical dish.

Unagi

A scrumptious dish of grilled Japanese eel, it is generally served in a rectangular box with a serving of rice.

Natto

A dish that you'll either hate or love! Despite its pungent smell, it is a healthy meal.

Gyoza

Pan fried dumplings which differ from Chinese dumplings with their typical pork, cabbage and mushroom fillings.

Japanese Kitchen Essentials

Staple Ingredients

  • Rice - Short-grain Japonica rice is most commonly used
  • Soy Sauce - Used for seasoning, it adds a deep umami flavour
  • Miso Paste - Fermented soybeans which add depth to soups and marinades
  • Dashi - A broth fullof umami, used as base for soups and dishes
  • Mirin - Sweet rice wine with a little sweetness, used in sauces and marinades
  • Sake - Alcoholic beverage vital in enhancing flavours and tenderizing meat.
  • Rice Vinegar - Mild vinegar essential for sushi rice

Cooking Techniques

  • Nimono (Simmering) - Slow cooking of ingredients in a seasoned broth
  • Mushimono (Steaming) - Used to preserve the natural flavours of delicate ingredients like vegetables and tofu
  • Grilling and Pan-Frying - Adds a smoky flavour and crispy exterior, balancing taste and texture
  • Sashimi (Raw Preparation) - Requires high quality ingredients and knife skills for a clean, fresh flavour
  • Tsukemono (Pickling) - Preserving vegetables in a mixture of vinegar, salt and sugar for a refreshing addition to dishes

My Personal Favorites and Opinions

1

Ramen

As a noodle, its already among my top ranks! Beyond that, the texture and thinness of the noodles are simply perfect. Additional ingredients like meat and egg add another layer of deliciousness and the depth of flavour only increases with the broth. It tastes and looks amazing, and will always be a go-to dish for cold and indecisive days!

2

Sushi

How can something so delicious exist? The ingredients are so simple but have so much flavour and freshness! Alone they taste amazing and with soy sauce - even more amazing! The salmon ones are exceptionally superb! If only I had the skill...I would be making them every week.

3

Unagi

I've only had a taste of it but it's already among my top 5 - this should be enough to make you go and try it. The eel is incredibly soft and tender but the slight crisp exterior just wraps it all up. The first bite will definitely make you go in for more - the balance between taste and texture really stands out in this dish.

4

Takoyaki

Although not mentioned on this page, these ball-shaped dumplings are definitely worth trying! The typical octopus filling is delicious but I would love to try other variations. They are a bit too filling to my liking as they can't be eaten as a light snack - but the flavour always makes up for it! They always turn out very hot inside, so you should be careful not to burn your tongue - as a piece of advice, don't eat them in one bite!

5

Karaage/Japanese Fried Chicken

Fried chicken can never go wrong and karaage is proof of that. The crunch is good, the taste is good, but maybe the oil is not too good. It's the lowest ranked simply because of the oil and, sometimes, fried chicken is too simple. It's easy to eat when there's nothing you particularly crave for and as a simple snack, but it doesn't have a lot of variety.
